I. Introduction

A. Rationale

On-the-Job Training gives students a chance to see how work really feels day to day. It is different from classroom exercises because the tasks are real, the time is limited, and people are counting on you. This practicum helped me understand workplace expectations, build confidence, and improve how I communicate while doing actual IT support work.

B. Work / Training / Project Expectations

My practicum goal was to improve technical skills and professional habits while supporting the daily IT operations of SISFU. I expected to learn faster troubleshooting, better documentation, and proper coordination when handling requests from different offices.

  • Apply IT knowledge in actual workplace tasks and workflows.
  • Strengthen documentation and communication skills.
  • Develop professionalism, accountability, and time management.
  • Improve troubleshooting and problem-solving abilities.

II. Organization Profile

A. Organization History and Background

It was Dr. Genevieve Ledesma-Laurel who founded in 1990 Southville International School and Colleges (SISC), which grew rapidly into a full international school. In 1997, Roger and Helen Bartholomew teamed up with the founder of SISC to start Southville International School Affiliated with Foreign Universities (SISFU), the first international university in the country. SISFU provides globally relevant education through partnerships with international institutions and rigorous academic standards.

Mission

SISFU, the premier transnational university in the Philippines, delivers globally-relevant quality undergraduate and graduate programs developed by accredited educational partners from the UK, Australia and the USA. Using rigorous international standards in instruction, assessment and research, supported by excellent facilities, highly-qualified faculty, and industry collaboration, SISFU prepares students to be globally competitive professionals, entrepreneurs, and leaders.

Vision

SISFU will be the leading transnational university that develops global leaders and professionals. It is distinguished by its excellent academic standards, outstanding instructional methodologies, relevant research, high levels of student achievement, a culture of innovation, and strong partnerships with ranked educational institutions.

B. Nature of Business

SISFU is a higher education institution offering transnational programs and student services, supported by administrative and technical departments to keep daily operations stable and efficient.

III. Training / Project Profile

A. Training / Project Description

My practicum assignment focused on technical assistance under the MIS/IT support environment. I supported staff, faculty, and students through troubleshooting, device setup, network assistance, documentation updates, and event technical preparation.

B. Nature of Training / Project

The training was hands-on and service-based. Tasks ranged from daily equipment readiness to solving real issues like printer connection errors, Wi-Fi concerns, and PC performance problems. I also supported office requests and event setups.

C. Training / Project Milestones

IV. Observations

A. Competitive Advantages

The workplace runs smoothly because tasks are organized, people communicate well, and responsibilities are clear. Even during busy hours, the staff coordinates and makes sure urgent concerns are handled first.

  • Clear procedures and a consistent daily routine.
  • Supportive staff and approachable supervisors.
  • Documentation habits that help tracking and follow-up.
  • Service mindset—requests are handled with urgency and respect.

B. Overall Observation

The practicum helped me become more confident in assisting users and handling real problems. I learned that technical skills are important, but being calm, respectful, and clear with people is just as important—especially when they are stressed and need help quickly.

V. Anecdotal Reports

E. Problems Encountered

Problem Situation / Impact Action Taken / Solution Result / Lesson
Unstable Wi-Fi / Internet Intermittent connection affected access and delayed office work. Checked device connections, did step-by-step isolation (device vs network), restarted properly, and coordinated with supervisor when needed. Improved response time; learned to troubleshoot calmly and systematically.
Printer Driver / Connection Issues Printers not detected or printing errors slowed down requests. Verified cables/network, reinstalled drivers when required, corrected default printer settings, and tested outputs before release. Restored printing; gained attention to detail and patience with settings.
Time-Sensitive Event Setup Equipment had to work before program start; pressure was high. Prepared early, tested cables and connections, kept backup options ready, and worked closely with the team during changes. Built confidence under pressure and learned to always test ahead.
Multiple Requests at the Same Time Support tasks, printing, and IDs could overlap and become overwhelming. Prioritized urgent concerns, queued non-urgent tasks, and communicated realistic updates to requesters. Better task management and more professional communication.

F. Salient Practices

Practice Observed Explanation Importance
Proper Documentation Requests and updates are recorded so tasks can be tracked and followed up properly. Prevents missed work and helps continuity even when the day gets busy.
Clear Communication Explaining steps to users and giving status updates to supervisors when needed. Reduces confusion and helps people feel supported, not ignored.
Preparation Before Events Testing equipment and connections ahead of time instead of waiting for the last minute. Prevents delays and makes events run smoother for everyone.
Professional User Support Being patient and respectful even when users are frustrated or in a hurry. Builds trust and keeps the working environment positive.
Accuracy in IDs and Documents Double-checking details before printing or releasing outputs. Saves time and avoids repeated work or unnecessary corrections.

H. Summary of Gained Experience

What I Gained

This practicum improved my confidence in troubleshooting, especially with common office issues like printers, Wi-Fi, and device setup. I learned how to communicate clearly, stay calm under pressure, and keep work organized even during busy days.

  • Faster troubleshooting and better checking habits
  • Improved communication and user support
  • Better prioritization when requests overlap
  • More confidence working in a professional environment

Why It Matters

The experience showed me that IT work is not only about fixing devices—it is also about helping people continue their work without stress. Even simple tasks like printing or connecting to Wi-Fi matter because they affect the entire day of the user.

Overall, the practicum prepared me for real workplace expectations and gave me a better understanding of how IT support operates daily.
