I. Introduction

Part I

A. Rationale

On-the-Job Training (OJT) is a real-world experience for students to learn about the field they plan to enter. It helps students understand different job environments, workplace culture, and the skills needed to perform effectively in professional settings. This experience allows students to apply classroom learning and improve their employability after graduation.

Internships help students learn, connect, and grow by providing opportunities to develop new skills, perform real tasks, and build professional networks that can support career opportunities.

B. Work / Training / Project Expectations

The practicum is expected to strengthen technical competence, professional discipline, teamwork, communication skills, and adaptability in real-world information technology environments.

  • Apply IT knowledge in actual workplace tasks.
  • Improve communication, documentation, and teamwork skills.
  • Develop professionalism (time management, ethics, accountability).
  • Enhance problem-solving and troubleshooting ability.

II. Organization Profile

Part II

A. Organization History and Background

It was Dr. Genevieve Ledesma-Laurel who founded in 1990 Southville International School and Colleges (SISC), which grew rapidly into a full international school. In 1997, Roger and Helen Bartholomew teamed up with the founder of SISC to start Southville International School Affiliated with Foreign Universities (SISFU), the first international university in the country.

SISFU aims to bring high quality education to the Philippines through globally relevant programs, rigorous international standards, excellent facilities, and strong international partnerships.

Mission and Vision

SISFU Mission

SISFU delivers globally-relevant quality undergraduate and graduate programs developed by accredited educational partners from the UK, Australia and the USA. Using rigorous international standards, supported by excellent facilities and faculty, SISFU prepares students to be globally competitive professionals, entrepreneurs, and leaders.

SISFU Vision

SISFU will be the leading transnational university that develops global leaders and professionals, known for excellent standards, innovation, relevant research, high achievement, and strong partnerships with ranked educational institutions.

B. Nature of Business

The institution operates as a higher education provider delivering undergraduate and graduate programs with international accreditation through partnerships with foreign universities.

Area Description
Core Service Transnational education, globally-relevant curricula, partner-accredited programs.
Key Stakeholders Students, faculty, partner universities, industry partners, community.
Focus Quality standards, international exposure, industry collaboration, outcomes-based learning.

III. Training / Project Profile

Part III

A. Training / Project Description

The practicum involved hands-on participation in IT support services, documentation, system maintenance, and administrative technical assistance. The experience enhanced real-world problem-solving skills and exposure to daily IT operations.

B. Nature of Training / Project

The training focused on practical application of information technology concepts, including troubleshooting, system support, user assistance, and data organization/documentation aligned with the organization’s workflow.

Training Area Examples
Technical Support Basic troubleshooting, user guidance, issue tracking.
Documentation Reports, forms, inventory lists, step-by-step procedures.
Systems & Maintenance System checks, updates, basic maintenance tasks.
Professional Skills Communication, teamwork, time management, task accountability.

C. Training / Project Milestones

Orientation and Familiarization Week 1

Understanding rules, tools, workplace processes, and assigned department setup.

Task Assignment and Supervision Week 2 onwards

Receiving daily/weekly tasks, learning proper documentation and technical support workflow.

Hands-on Execution Mid-term

Performing actual support tasks, assisting users, completing documentation and maintenance activities.

Completion and Evaluation End of Practicum

Submission of outputs, performance evaluation, reflection, and portfolio/report finalization.
